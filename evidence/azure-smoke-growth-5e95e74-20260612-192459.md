# Media Plan - MIRA

## Executive Summary
MIRA should treat this as a controlled growth plan, not a simple scale-up of current campaigns. The fixed budget context increases investment from current GA4 spend of $3,210 to $10,000, but the GA4-backed fitted channels are already saturated, so the plan preserves demand capture while shifting incremental learning into Meta and TikTok tests plus a gated reserve. Google and LinkedIn remain important, but their role is efficiency, quality, and diagnostic improvement rather than volume expansion. Success should be judged on qualified pipeline, CPQL, and opportunity quality rather than lead volume or low-cost engagement.

## Audience Strategy
Prioritize B2B marketers showing observable buying intent: high-intent search behavior, site engagement, content consumption, demo-page visits, and CRM-matched account activity. LinkedIn should narrow to firmographic precision, ABM, persona validation, and retargeting rather than broad prospecting. Existing customers and lifecycle-stage audiences should be excluded from cold acquisition and used only for retargeting or nurture. CRM segments by company size, industry, region, and lifecycle stage should remain directional because several segments are sparse and need more conversion volume before they can guide optimization confidently.

## Channel Rationale
The plan keeps Google as the core demand-capture layer while moving it from scale mode to efficiency diagnostics: query pruning, negative keyword work, and tighter high-intent structure. LinkedIn remains the B2B precision layer, but spend should concentrate on the strongest ICP, account, persona, and retargeting use cases until CPQL and opportunity quality justify reinvestment. Meta and TikTok should not be added to the deterministic allocation table because they do not have GA4 spend history here; they should be treated as qualitative expansion tests for demand creation, retargeting, and workflow-led creative learning. The reserve should stay gated and be released only when qualified pipeline or CPQL evidence supports incremental scaling.

## Expansion Opportunities
Meta is the stronger near-term expansion candidate for structured creative, retargeting, lookalike, and pain-point testing among B2B marketers, provided creative velocity is sufficient. TikTok should be scoped as a learning test for workflow education, problem-solution storytelling, and creator-style narratives, with downstream quality checks before any scale decision. Both channels should remain outside the deterministic allocation table until they build GA4 spend history and conversion evidence. If either expansion test proves qualified pipeline quality, the reserve can be released into the best-performing opportunity.

## Sequencing & Timing
Start by protecting Google demand capture and tightening search efficiency while narrowing LinkedIn to ICP, ABM, and retargeting use cases. Launch Meta and TikTok as separate phase-one learning tracks with clear hypotheses, creative themes, UTMs, and pipeline-quality measurement. Review early signals against qualified pipeline and CPQL gates before moving reserve dollars. Only after a channel demonstrates downstream quality should MIRA expand budgets or re-open broader prospecting.

## Risks & Assumptions
The main risk is misreading channel saturation as only a media issue; Google and LinkedIn under-scaling may also reflect landing-page, offer, query, audience, or funnel constraints. Meta and TikTok may create inexpensive engagement without qualified pipeline if creative and targeting do not map to buying intent. Sparse CRM segments reduce confidence in company-size, industry, region, and lifecycle-stage optimization, so overfitting audience decisions would be premature. TikTok should be especially protected from vanity-metric optimization, and weak creative production should pause scaling on creative-hungry channels.
