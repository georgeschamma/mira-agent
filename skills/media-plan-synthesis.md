# Media Plan Synthesis Guidelines

These guidelines assist the synthesis agent in analyzing performance, audience, and research inputs to generate a structured B2B media plan brief.

## B2B Media Planning Framework

### 1. Channel Roles
Classify each channel into one of the following roles:
- **harvest**: High marginal ROI channels where demand is already present (e.g. Paid Search/CPC). Focus on capturing high-intent traffic.
- **nurture**: Mid-funnel channels for educating and building relationship (e.g. targeted LinkedIn content, retargeting).
- **test**: Low-confidence or new channels where we lack historical performance data but have strong audience alignment (e.g. TikTok, Meta prospecting).
- **hold**: Channels that have reached saturation and weak marginal returns where we maintain current spend but do not scale up.

### 2. Planning Modes
Align channel spend priorities with the target planning mode:
- **efficiency**: Focus heavily on *harvest* and *hold* channels. Cut lowest mROI first. Limit or eliminate *test* budgets.
- **growth**: Prioritize scaling *nurture* and launching *test* channels to capture new audience segments, using the expansion budget.
- **balanced**: Equal weight to capturing current demand and testing future growth channels.

### 3. Spend Waterfall
Rank order spend allocation priorities:
1. Lock in the baseline spend for stable channels that have insufficient or optimal data but demonstrate clear strategic value.
2. Fund highest-performing fitted channels up to their optimal zones.
3. Redirect capped/unallocated funds to structured, hyposthesis-driven expansion tests.
