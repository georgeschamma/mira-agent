# Media Plan - MIRA

## Executive Summary
MIRA should use the $10,000 budget to protect high-intent demand capture, reduce spend where saturation is clearest, and stage expansion into Meta and TikTok as controlled tests. The fixed table keeps Google paid search at $1,100 and reduces LinkedIn paid to $640. The remaining expansion structure supports Meta and TikTok launches, but because those channels lack GA4 history, they should be discussed as qualitative test candidates rather than inserted into the deterministic allocation table.

## Audience Strategy
Prioritize B2B marketers with separate paths for cold prospecting, warm retargeting, and CRM-matched audiences. Google should capture active demand from marketers searching for solutions, while LinkedIn should be used selectively for firmographic precision, ABM, retargeting, and higher-value account segments. Customers should be excluded from cold prospecting and used only for retargeting, upsell, or nurture journeys. CRM segment reads by company size, industry, lifecycle stage, and region should remain directional because the available segment counts are sparse.

## Channel Rationale
The plan protects proven intent capture while avoiding inefficient scale in saturated incumbents. Google paid search is held at $1,100 to maintain high-intent demand capture, with emphasis on query quality, negative keywords, and offline conversion feedback. LinkedIn is reduced to $640 because it is saturated and has the lower marginal ROI, so it should be reserved for senior marketers, firmographic precision, and account-based use cases. Meta and TikTok are not added to the deterministic allocation table because they lack GA4 spend history; instead, they should be treated as narrative-only expansion tests using the fixed expansion structure from the brief: Meta at a $3,300 phase 1 test plus $200 staged reserve, and TikTok at a $3,300 phase 1 test plus $200 staged reserve.

## Expansion Opportunities
Meta is the strongest near-term expansion candidate for scaled reach, creative testing, retargeting, and potentially lower-cost qualified lead generation among B2B marketers outside LinkedIn-only audiences. TikTok should be framed as an earlier-funnel discovery test using educational and problem-aware creative, with success measured by qualified lead rate, engaged landing-page visits, and its ability to feed retargeting pools. The unassigned reserve pool of $1,260 should stay gated until expansion campaigns show qualified pipeline contribution or credible leading indicators.

## Sequencing & Timing
Start by holding Google steady at $1,100 and tightening search quality through negatives, query review, and offline conversion feedback. Reduce LinkedIn to $640 and focus it on firmographic precision, retargeting, and higher-value account segments rather than broad scale. Launch Meta and TikTok as structured expansion tests with cold, warm, and CRM audiences separated from the start. Release staged reserves only after KPI gates show qualified CPL, qualified lead rate, engaged visits, or pipeline-sourced opportunities moving in the right direction.

## Risks & Assumptions
The main risk is that Meta and TikTok produce cheaper engagement or leads without matching sales qualification, so form filters, landing-page message match, and CRM feedback are required. Sparse CRM segment data limits confidence in company-size, industry, lifecycle, and regional prioritization. Scaling Google or LinkedIn beyond the supported plan could dilute marginal efficiency because both incumbent channels are saturated. TikTok may also show a longer lag between engagement and pipeline impact, and attribution may understate upper-funnel value unless offline conversions and retargeting paths are tracked.
